What Is PRP Therapy?

PRP, or platelet-rich plasma therapy, uses a concentrated portion of your own blood, rich in platelets and growth factors, to stimulate your skin's natural regeneration process. A small blood sample is drawn, processed in a centrifuge to separate and concentrate the platelet-rich plasma, and then reintroduced into the treatment area, typically via microneedling or targeted injection. Because the treatment uses your own biological material rather than a synthetic filler or product, it's often described as one of the more natural options in medical aesthetics.

At Velora Dental and Aesthetic Clinic, PRP therapy is administered by Dr. Fatima Malak, whose medical aesthetics training means the treatment is delivered with a clear understanding of skin biology, not just a cosmetic procedure performed on autopilot. The blood draw, processing, and application are all handled by the same clinician, so there's no handoff between different staff at different stages of a treatment that involves your own blood.

What PRP Therapy Is Used For

PRP is commonly used to improve overall skin texture, tone, and radiance, and is often chosen by patients dealing with early signs of ageing, acne scarring, or generally lacklustre skin looking for a more natural approach than fillers or more invasive procedures. It's also used in combination with microneedling, where the PRP is applied to the skin during the micro-channel creation process, allowing it to absorb more effectively.

Results build gradually, since the treatment works by encouraging your body's own regenerative process rather than delivering an instant, dramatic change, which is worth understanding clearly before starting a course. Patients often describe the improvement as their skin looking generally healthier and more resilient over time, rather than one specific feature changing overnight.

Common Reasons Patients Choose PRP

  • Improving overall skin texture and tone
  • Addressing acne scarring gradually
  • Early signs of ageing and loss of radiance
  • Wanting a treatment based on your own biology rather than synthetic products

As with any aesthetic treatment, the right fit depends on your specific skin and goals, which is why PRP is discussed at consultation alongside other options rather than presented as a universal solution for every concern.

What Happens During a PRP Session

Your session begins with a small blood draw, similar to a routine blood test. This sample is then processed in a centrifuge, which separates the platelet-rich plasma from the rest of the blood, typically taking around 10 to 15 minutes. Once ready, the PRP is applied to the treatment area, usually via fine microneedling or targeted injection, depending on the goal and area being treated.

The full appointment, including the blood draw, processing time, and application, typically takes 45 to 60 minutes. Because the plasma comes from your own blood, the risk of allergic reaction is minimal compared to synthetic alternatives. Some patients choose to combine this visit with a related consultation, such as discussing HydraFacial, to make the most of a single trip to the clinic.

Results and Recovery

Mild redness for 24 to 48 hours after treatment is normal and expected, similar to what you'd see after microneedling alone. Results build gradually over the following weeks as your skin's regenerative process responds to the growth factors introduced, which is why PRP is usually recommended as a course of several sessions rather than a single visit, for patients seeking a meaningful, lasting improvement.

Because it's a physician-administered treatment using your own biological material, PRP is generally considered a low-risk option within medical aesthetics, though as with any treatment, your suitability is confirmed at consultation based on your health history.

Setting Realistic Expectations

PRP is not a treatment that produces the same immediate, dramatic change as a filler or more invasive procedure, and it's worth going in with that understanding. Its strength lies in gradually improving your skin's own quality over time, texture, tone, and resilience, rather than instantly altering a specific feature. Patients who do best with PRP tend to be those looking for a natural-feeling improvement they can maintain over months and years, not a one-time dramatic transformation.

This is exactly the kind of honest expectation-setting Dr. Fatima prioritises at your first consultation, so you can decide whether PRP fits what you're actually looking for, or whether a different treatment, or combination of treatments, would serve your goals better.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is PRP therapy safe?

Yes, generally. Because the plasma is drawn from your own blood, the risk of allergic reaction is minimal compared to synthetic treatments. Your suitability is still confirmed at consultation based on your health history.

How many PRP sessions will I need?

Results build gradually, so a course of several sessions is typically recommended for meaningful, lasting improvement, rather than a single visit. Dr. Fatima will suggest a schedule based on your specific goals.

Is there downtime after PRP therapy?

Mild redness for 24 to 48 hours is normal and expected, similar to microneedling. Most patients resume normal activities the same or next day.

Does PRP therapy hurt?

A topical numbing option can be discussed if you're concerned about discomfort. Most patients tolerate the treatment well, describing mild discomfort during application rather than significant pain.

Can PRP be combined with other treatments?

Yes, PRP is often combined with microneedling or HydraFacial as part of a broader skin improvement plan, which Dr. Fatima can discuss during your consultation.

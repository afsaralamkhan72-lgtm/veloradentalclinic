What Are Dental Veneers?

Dental veneers are thin, custom-shaped shells, usually made from porcelain or composite resin, bonded to the front surface of a tooth. They're used to change the appearance of teeth that are chipped, unevenly shaped, slightly gapped, or discoloured in a way that whitening alone can't correct. Because each veneer is shaped and colour-matched individually, the result at Velora Dental and Aesthetic Clinic is designed to look like your own natural teeth, just refined, rather than an obviously artificial "veneer smile."

Dr. Fatima Malak plans veneer cases around your whole smile, not just the single tooth you may be concerned about, so the proportions and shade work together naturally when you speak or smile.

What Veneers Can and Can't Fix

Veneers are well suited to correcting chipped edges, minor gaps between front teeth, uneven or worn tooth edges, and discolouration that hasn't responded to whitening, such as staining from certain childhood medications. They can also subtly improve the appearance of a slightly misshapen tooth without the months of treatment that orthodontics would require.

What veneers are not designed to fix is a significantly misaligned bite or major crowding, both of which are better addressed with orthodontic treatment first. Veneers also require healthy underlying teeth; if there's active decay or gum disease, this is treated before veneers are considered, not covered up by them.

Common Reasons Patients Choose Veneers

  • A chipped or worn front tooth affecting confidence when smiling
  • Small gaps between the front teeth
  • Staining that hasn't responded to professional whitening
  • Teeth that are naturally uneven in shape or length

Porcelain vs Composite Veneers

Porcelain veneers are more durable and resistant to staining over time, and are custom-fabricated to fit your tooth precisely, usually requiring two visits. Composite veneers are sculpted directly onto the tooth in a single visit, making them a quicker and generally more affordable option, though they don't tend to last quite as long or resist staining as well as porcelain. Which one makes sense for you depends on your budget, the specific issue being corrected, and how long you want the result to last, all of which Dr. Fatima will walk through honestly at your consultation.

Some patients choose a mix, porcelain for the teeth most visible when smiling and composite elsewhere, as a way of balancing cost against durability. This kind of practical trade-off is exactly the sort of thing worth discussing openly at your first visit, rather than assuming one material is automatically "better" for every tooth in your mouth.

The Veneer Process at Velora

Your first visit is a consultation where Dr. Fatima examines your teeth, discusses what you'd like to change, and confirms whether veneers are the right tool for that goal. For porcelain veneers, a small amount of enamel is typically prepared on the tooth surface to make room for the shell, an impression is taken, and a temporary veneer may be fitted while your custom veneer is made. At your next visit, the permanent veneer is bonded into place, checked for fit and bite, and polished.

For composite veneers, the resin is applied, shaped, and cured directly onto the tooth in a single sitting, so you leave with the finished result the same day.

Caring for Your Veneers

Veneers are cared for like natural teeth: regular brushing, flossing, and routine check-ups. Porcelain veneers resist staining well, but composite veneers can pick up staining over time from tea, coffee, or tobacco, similar to natural enamel. Avoiding using your front teeth to bite down on very hard objects, such as ice or pen caps, helps protect veneers from chipping, regardless of which material you choose.

Grinding your teeth at night is one of the more common reasons veneers chip prematurely. If you have a history of teeth grinding, this is worth mentioning at your consultation, since a simple night guard can meaningfully extend the life of your veneers and protect your natural teeth at the same time.

Why a Consultation Matters Before Committing

Veneers are a cosmetic investment, and the best results come from a plan that considers your whole smile rather than a single tooth in isolation. A rushed consultation that skips imaging or a full assessment of your bite can result in veneers that look fine individually but don't sit well together, or that fail prematurely because an underlying issue, like a misaligned bite, was never addressed. Dr. Fatima's approach is to assess the full picture first, even if that means recommending a different treatment, or a combination of treatments, instead of veneers alone.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do dental veneers last?

Porcelain veneers typically last 10 to 15 years or longer with good care, while composite veneers generally last a shorter period, often 5 to 7 years, before needing replacement or touch-up.

Do veneers require removing a lot of tooth structure?

Porcelain veneers require a small amount of enamel preparation, generally minimal and confined to the front surface. Composite veneers often require little to no removal of tooth structure.

Can veneers fix crooked teeth?

Veneers can subtly improve the appearance of minor unevenness, but significant misalignment is better addressed with orthodontic treatment first. Dr. Fatima will advise honestly on which approach fits your case.

Will my veneers look natural?

Yes, that's the goal. Veneers are shaped and shade-matched individually to blend with your other teeth and your overall smile, rather than looking uniformly white and artificial.

Can veneers stain?

Porcelain veneers are highly stain-resistant. Composite veneers can pick up some surface staining over time, similar to natural teeth, which is worth considering if you drink a lot of tea or coffee.

Curious Whether Veneers Are Right for You?

Bring your concerns, whether it's a chip, a gap, or overall shape, and Dr. Fatima will assess whether veneers are the best fit or whether another option would serve you better.

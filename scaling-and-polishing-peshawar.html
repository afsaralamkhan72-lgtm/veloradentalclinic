What Is Scaling and Polishing?

Scaling and polishing is a routine but important dental cleaning that removes plaque and tartar, the hardened deposits that regular brushing at home can't remove on its own, from both above and below the gumline. At Velora Dental and Aesthetic Clinic, this is performed using ultrasonic and hand instruments to gently and thoroughly clear these deposits, followed by a polishing step that smooths the tooth surface and lifts surface staining.

It's one of the simplest, lowest-risk treatments in dentistry, and also one of the most important for preventing bigger problems down the line, since untreated tartar buildup is a leading contributor to gum disease. Because the treatment is quick, comfortable, and requires no recovery time, there's very little reason to put it off once it's due.

Why Plaque and Tartar Removal Matters

Plaque is a soft, sticky film of bacteria that forms on teeth constantly, which is why daily brushing and flossing matter so much. Left in place, plaque hardens into tartar (also called calculus) within as little as a day or two, and once hardened, tartar can no longer be removed with a toothbrush, only by a dental professional. Tartar buildup, especially below the gumline, irritates gum tissue and is one of the earliest steps toward gum disease, which left unaddressed can eventually affect the bone supporting your teeth.

Regular scaling and polishing interrupts this cycle before it progresses, which is why it's recommended as a routine visit rather than something to book only when a problem is already visible.

Signs You May Be Overdue for a Cleaning

  • Gums that bleed when you brush or floss
  • A rough or gritty feeling on the back of your lower front teeth
  • Persistent bad breath despite regular brushing
  • Visible yellow or brown deposits near the gumline

None of these signs are things to wait out on their own. Bleeding gums in particular are often dismissed as normal, especially if it's been happening for a while, when in fact it's usually the earliest and most reversible signal that plaque and tartar buildup need professional attention.

What Happens During a Scaling and Polishing Visit

Dr. Fatima or her team first examines your teeth and gums to assess how much buildup is present and whether there are any early signs of gum disease worth flagging. Ultrasonic instruments are then used to break up and remove tartar deposits, followed by hand instruments for more precise cleaning around the gumline and between teeth. Once the deposits are cleared, a polishing paste is used to smooth the tooth surface, which also helps lift surface staining from tea, coffee, or tobacco.

The whole process typically takes 30 to 45 minutes and requires no anaesthesia. Some patients feel mild sensitivity during the ultrasonic step, particularly if there's a significant buildup being removed for the first time in a while, but this is brief and not typically described as painful.

Aftercare and Maintaining Results

Mild sensitivity for a day afterward is normal, especially to cold, and settles quickly. There's no real downtime: you can eat, drink, and resume normal activity immediately after your appointment. To maintain the results, the same daily habits apply as always, brushing twice a day, flossing or using an interdental brush, and avoiding excessive sugary or acidic foods that accelerate plaque formation.

Most patients benefit from scaling and polishing roughly every six months, though this can vary; some patients with a tendency toward faster tartar buildup are advised to come in more frequently, something Dr. Fatima will flag if it applies to you.

What Happens if Gum Disease Is Left Untreated

Early gum inflammation, known as gingivitis, is reversible with a professional cleaning and improved home care. Left untreated over months or years, however, it can progress to periodontitis, a more serious form of gum disease that affects the bone and tissue supporting your teeth, and can eventually lead to tooth loss. Because this progression is often painless in its early stages, regular scaling and polishing visits are one of the simplest ways to catch it while it's still easily reversible, rather than waiting for symptoms that only appear once the disease has advanced.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is scaling and polishing painful?

No. Most patients feel at most mild sensitivity during the ultrasonic cleaning step, particularly if there's significant tartar buildup. It's not typically described as a painful procedure.

How often should I get scaling and polishing done?

Every six months is a common recommendation for most patients, though Dr. Fatima may suggest more frequent visits if you're prone to faster plaque or tartar buildup.

Will scaling and polishing whiten my teeth?

It removes surface staining and buildup, which often makes teeth look noticeably brighter, but it isn't a substitute for professional whitening if you're looking to change your teeth's underlying shade.

Can I eat right after a cleaning?

Yes. There's no downtime, and you can eat, drink, and go about your day immediately after your appointment.

Why do my gums bleed when I brush?

This is often an early sign of gum inflammation caused by plaque and tartar buildup near the gumline. A scaling and polishing session, combined with improved home care, usually resolves this within a couple of weeks.

If it's been more than six months since your last visit, book a scaling and polishing session and let Dr. Fatima check your gum health while she's at it.

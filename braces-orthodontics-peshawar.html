What Orthodontic Treatment Involves

Orthodontic treatment gradually moves teeth into a straighter, better-aligned position using controlled, sustained pressure, typically through braces or clear alignment options. At Velora Dental and Aesthetic Clinic, treatment is planned by Dr. Fatima Malak based on full imaging and an assessment of your bite, not just how your teeth look at a glance, since alignment affects chewing function and long-term dental health, not only appearance. A bite that's out of alignment can also contribute to uneven wear on specific teeth over the years, jaw discomfort, and even headaches in some patients, which is why orthodontic assessment looks beyond simple cosmetics.

Treatment options are discussed based on your specific case, lifestyle, and preferences, whether that's traditional braces for a straightforward correction or a more discreet option for adult patients who want a less visible treatment during work or social settings.

Braces for Children vs Adults

For children, orthodontic treatment is often timed around jaw growth and the eruption of permanent teeth, and can sometimes prevent more complex issues from developing later. An early assessment, generally recommended once most adult teeth have come in, helps identify whether intervention now would simplify treatment down the line.

For adults, orthodontic treatment is just as viable, and increasingly common. Jaw growth is no longer a factor, but teeth can still be moved effectively at any age, provided the gums and supporting bone are healthy. Many adult patients come in because they either never had orthodontic treatment as a child, or their teeth have shifted over the years, sometimes after a missing tooth was never replaced.

Common Reasons Patients Seek Orthodontic Treatment

  • Crowded, overlapping, or gapped teeth
  • A bite that doesn't align comfortably, causing uneven wear
  • Teeth that have shifted over time, especially after tooth loss
  • A child whose adult teeth are coming in visibly crowded or crooked

Whatever the reason, the goal of assessment is the same: to understand not just what you'd like to change, but what's realistic given your bite, jaw, and the position of your roots, so the treatment plan you agree to is one that will actually hold long-term.

What to Expect: Consultation to Retainer

Your first visit involves a full assessment and imaging so Dr. Fatima can see not just the visible position of your teeth but the roots and jaw alignment beneath. From there, she discusses which options suit your bite and goals, along with a realistic timeline, since this varies significantly depending on how much movement is needed.

Once treatment begins, you'll have regular adjustment visits, typically monthly, where the braces are tightened or adjusted to continue guiding the teeth into position. These visits are also a chance to flag any discomfort or loose brackets early, rather than living with an issue until the next scheduled appointment.

When treatment is complete, retainers are provided to hold the teeth in their new position. Teeth have a natural tendency to drift back toward their original position over time, so wearing retainers as instructed is what protects the result you've just spent months achieving. Retainers may be fixed (a thin wire bonded behind the teeth) or removable, and Dr. Fatima will recommend the option best suited to your case and how consistently you're likely to wear a removable one if given the choice.

Choosing the Right Type of Treatment

Not every case needs the same approach. Traditional metal braces remain a reliable, often more affordable option for complex movements, while ceramic or more discreet alternatives suit patients who want a less visible treatment during work or social situations. Some minor cases may be suited to clear aligner-style options. The right choice depends on the specific correction needed, your budget, and how much visibility matters to you day to day, all of which is discussed honestly at your assessment rather than defaulting to whichever option is most profitable to prescribe.

Living With Braces Day to Day

There's a short adjustment period after each fitting or tightening, where the teeth feel tender, typically for a day or two. Beyond that, daily life continues largely as normal, though some care around sticky or very hard foods helps avoid damaging brackets or wires. Oral hygiene becomes slightly more involved with braces in place, and Dr. Fatima's team will walk you through the best way to clean effectively around brackets to avoid staining or decay once treatment is complete.

Playing contact sports or a musical instrument that involves the mouth is still possible with braces, though a properly fitted mouthguard is recommended for sports to protect both the braces and your teeth. This is exactly the kind of practical, day-to-day question worth raising at your consultation rather than guessing afterward.

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get braces as an adult?

Yes. Orthodontic treatment at Velora is offered for children and adults alike, with options suited to different lifestyles. Jaw growth isn't required for teeth to move effectively at any age.

How long does orthodontic treatment take?

Typically 12 to 24 months, though this varies significantly based on how much correction is needed. Your assessment will give you a realistic timeline for your specific case rather than a generic estimate.

Do braces hurt?

There's usually a short adjustment period of tenderness after each fitting or tightening, lasting a day or two. Beyond that, day-to-day discomfort is minimal.

At what age should my child first see an orthodontist?

A general guideline is once most of a child's adult teeth have come in, though Dr. Fatima can advise on timing specific to your child during a consultation.

Will I need to wear a retainer after braces come off?

Yes. Retainers hold your teeth in their new position, since teeth naturally drift back toward their original alignment over time without one.

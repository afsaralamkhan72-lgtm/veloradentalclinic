What Is a Dental Implant?

A dental implant is a small titanium post placed into the jawbone to act as an artificial tooth root. Once it has integrated with the bone, a custom-made crown is fitted on top, giving you a replacement tooth that looks, feels, and functions like your own. Unlike a bridge, an implant does not rely on the neighbouring teeth for support, and unlike a removable denture, it stays fixed in place permanently.

At Velora Dental and Aesthetic Clinic in Phase 3, Peshawar, implant placement is carried out by Dr. Fatima Malak, who holds C-Implant certification specifically in this procedure, in addition to her core dental qualifications. Every case begins with imaging to assess bone density and plan the implant's position with precision before anything is placed.

Who Is a Candidate for Dental Implants?

Dental implants are most commonly considered by patients who are missing one or more teeth due to decay, injury, or extraction, and want a long-term, fixed solution rather than a removable one. Good candidates generally have healthy gums and adequate bone volume in the jaw, though bone grafting can sometimes create suitability where it didn't previously exist. Age alone is rarely a barrier; what matters more is overall oral and general health.

If you currently wear a partial or full denture and find it uncomfortable, unstable, or simply want a more permanent alternative, an implant-supported restoration is worth discussing at a consultation. The same applies if you've lost a tooth and are hesitant about grinding down healthy neighbouring teeth for a traditional bridge.

Signs You May Want to Discuss Implants

  • A missing tooth that you've been avoiding replacing
  • A loose or failing bridge that needs a longer-term solution
  • Denture discomfort, slipping, or difficulty chewing
  • Bone loss in the jaw following a long-standing gap

The Dental Implant Process at Velora

Your first visit is a consultation and assessment. Dr. Fatima reviews your dental and medical history, examines the site, and takes imaging to assess the bone available. This is also when she'll explain whether your case is straightforward or whether preparatory steps, such as a bone graft, are advisable first.

On the day of placement, the implant post is fitted under local anaesthesia, a process that typically takes under an hour for a single implant. What follows is a healing period, usually a few months, during which the implant fuses with the surrounding bone in a process called osseointegration. Once healing is confirmed, a custom crown, colour-matched to your natural teeth, is fitted on top, completing the restoration.

Throughout this process, you'll have scheduled check-ins so that healing can be monitored and any concerns addressed early, rather than waiting until the final fitting to discover a problem.

Benefits of Choosing Dental Implants

The main appeal of implants is permanence: they are designed to function like a natural tooth root, supporting a crown that doesn't need to be removed, soaked, or replaced every few years. Because the implant integrates with the jawbone, it also helps preserve bone volume in the area, something that gaps and dentures don't do as effectively over time.

Implants don't require altering the teeth on either side of the gap, which is a meaningful advantage over a traditional bridge. Day to day, most patients find that a well-integrated implant looks, feels, and functions closely enough to a natural tooth that they stop thinking about it as a "replacement" at all.

Caring for Your Dental Implant

An implant-supported tooth is cared for much like a natural one: regular brushing, flossing or an interdental brush around the crown, and routine scaling and polishing visits to keep the surrounding gum tissue healthy. Because implants don't decay the way natural teeth can, the main long-term risk is gum inflammation around the implant site, which is why maintaining regular check-ups matters even after the restoration is complete.

Most patients find that once healing is finished, an implant requires no special routine beyond good general oral hygiene. Avoiding excessive force on the crown, such as chewing ice or very hard foods, helps protect the restoration over the long term, the same advice that applies to natural teeth.

What Affects the Cost of Dental Implants

Implant cost varies from patient to patient, depending on factors such as the number of teeth being replaced, whether a bone graft is needed beforehand, the type of crown material used, and the complexity of your specific case. Rather than quote a generic figure that may not apply to your situation, Dr. Fatima assesses your case at consultation and walks you through the costs involved for your specific treatment plan, with no obligation to proceed the same day.

Frequently Asked Questions

How long do dental implants last?

With proper oral hygiene and regular check-ups, dental implants can last decades, and in many cases a lifetime. The crown on top may eventually need replacement due to normal wear, but the implant post itself is designed for long-term, permanent use.

Is getting a dental implant painful?

The placement procedure is performed under local anaesthesia, so you shouldn't feel pain during the process. Mild soreness or swelling for a few days afterward is normal and manageable with routine aftercare guidance.

How long does the full dental implant process take?

Placement itself takes about 60–90 minutes per implant, but the full process, including bone healing before the crown is fitted, typically spans a few months. Your exact timeline depends on your bone health and healing response.

Am I too old for a dental implant?

Age itself is rarely a barrier. What matters more is bone health, gum health, and general medical history, all of which are assessed during your consultation with Dr. Fatima.

What happens if I don't replace a missing tooth?

Gaps left untreated can lead to shifting of neighbouring teeth, bite changes, and gradual bone loss in the jaw at that site. Replacing a missing tooth, whether with an implant or another option, helps prevent these knock-on effects.

What Is Professional Teeth Whitening?

Professional teeth whitening uses a clinical-strength whitening gel, applied under supervision, to lift staining and discolouration built up from tea, coffee, tobacco, certain medications, or simply age. Unlike over-the-counter strips or kits bought online, a supervised session at Velora Dental and Aesthetic Clinic starts with a shade assessment and a check of your gum and enamel health, so the treatment is suited to your mouth specifically, not a generic one-size-fits-all product. This upfront check is the main difference between a clinical session and a drugstore kit, and it's the reason results tend to be both stronger and more even across all your teeth.

Dr. Fatima Malak oversees every whitening session personally, protecting the gums before the gel is applied and monitoring the result as it develops, so the outcome is both safe and predictable. This matters because whitening gel left unmonitored on already-sensitive teeth or exposed gum tissue can cause unnecessary irritation, something a home kit has no way of checking for before you use it.

Who Should Consider Teeth Whitening?

Whitening is generally suited to anyone with healthy teeth and gums who wants to address surface staining or general yellowing that has built up over time. It's a popular choice before a wedding, an important event, or simply as a refresh. It is not a treatment for existing dental disease, so if you have untreated decay, gum disease, or significant sensitivity, these are addressed first, as whitening works best on a healthy foundation.

It's worth noting that whitening lightens your natural tooth enamel, but does not change the colour of existing crowns, veneers, or fillings. If you have visible restorations at the front of your mouth, this is discussed at your consultation so the result looks even and natural.

Patients with a history of very sensitive teeth are not automatically excluded from whitening, but this is exactly the kind of detail worth flagging honestly at your consultation, rather than discovering it mid-session. Dr. Fatima can adjust the approach, including gel strength and session length, based on what your teeth can comfortably tolerate.

Common Reasons Patients Choose Whitening

  • Staining from tea, coffee, or tobacco use over the years
  • General yellowing that has developed gradually with age

What Happens During a Whitening Session

Your visit begins with a shade assessment, comparing your current tooth colour against a standard shade guide so progress can be measured objectively. Your gums are then protected with a barrier, since the whitening gel is strong enough to irritate soft tissue if left unprotected. The gel is applied to the front surfaces of your teeth and left to work for a set period, sometimes with the aid of a curing light, before being removed and reapplied for additional cycles depending on your goals.

Many patients see a visible difference after a single session, though the exact degree of change depends on your starting shade and the nature of the staining. For more significant discolouration, Dr. Fatima may recommend a short course of sessions rather than promising results that a single visit can't reliably deliver.

Aftercare and Maintaining Your Results

It's normal to experience some sensitivity to cold for a day or two after whitening, which settles on its own. Avoiding heavily pigmented foods and drinks, such as tea, coffee, red wine, and turmeric-based dishes, for the first 24 to 48 hours helps the result last longer, since the enamel is slightly more porous immediately after treatment.

Whitening results are not permanent. With normal eating and drinking habits, most patients find a touch-up session every year or so keeps their smile at the shade they want, and this is something you can schedule at Velora as needed rather than starting the whole process from scratch each time.

Whitening vs Other Cosmetic Options

Whitening is often the simplest and most affordable first step when a smile looks dull or discoloured, since it works with your existing tooth structure rather than changing it. If staining is combined with chips, gaps, or uneven tooth shape, whitening alone won't address those issues, and Dr. Fatima may discuss veneers as a complementary or alternative option depending on what you're trying to achieve. The two are not mutually exclusive: many patients whiten first, then decide whether veneers are still something they want once they see how much a professional whitening session alone improves things.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is teeth whitening safe?

Yes, when performed under professional supervision. Your gums are protected before the whitening gel is applied, and Dr. Fatima checks your teeth and gums beforehand to confirm whitening is appropriate for your specific case.

How long do whitening results last?

This varies by patient and lifestyle, but most people maintain their result for around a year before wanting a touch-up, especially if they drink a lot of tea, coffee, or similarly staining drinks.

Will whitening work on crowns or fillings?

No. Whitening only lightens natural tooth enamel, not existing restorations. If you have visible crowns, veneers, or fillings at the front of your mouth, this is discussed at your consultation so the final look is even.

Does teeth whitening hurt?

Most patients feel little to nothing during the session itself. Some experience mild sensitivity to cold for a day or two afterward, which is normal and settles on its own.

How many sessions will I need?

Many patients see a visible result after a single session. More significant staining may benefit from a short course, which Dr. Fatima will recommend honestly based on your starting shade rather than upselling unnecessary visits.
